ABOUT

Amelia Kim (b. 2003) is an artist based in New York City. She studied at the School of the Art Institute of Chicago.

Amelia builds worlds from repetition — thousands of ink dots accumulating into dense, coiling forms that read as both organism and machine. A self-taught coder and interdisciplinary artist working across drawing, soft sculpture, installation, and web-based art, she approaches every new medium as a room to inhabit. Amelia's pointillism drawings morph under the eye: spiraling shells become neural networks, seed pods dissolve into gears, ghost-like faces surface and retreat. Her practice is both meditative and mischievous — she spends hours in the trance of mark-making, then hand-sews stuffed creatures with drawn-on eyes and invites others to use them to tear down walls. She codes websites that feel less like pages and more like places. Amelia's environments are organic but alien, tender but strange — the slowest gestures holding the most life.
